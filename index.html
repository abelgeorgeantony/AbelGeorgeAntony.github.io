About me

I am Abel George Antony, just a thinker. From a young age I've been fascinated by technology. This interest led me down the path to choose computer science in 11th standard which in turn opened the world of programming, where I've discovered a realm of endless possibilities. I am not currently an expert in any domains, but after learning the basics of programming I have become confident that I can learn pretty much any technology from scratch.

First steps
My journey as a programmer began in 11th standard when I had no prior knowledge of programming and limited understanding of computers. However, I was always fascinated by technology, which led me to choose the computer science stream in school. Around the same time, I stumbled upon an advertisement in a video for the 100k coding challenge by the channel Brototype (previously known as Crossroads). Intrigued by the idea, I watched the first video and signed up for the challenge. Unfortunately, without access to a computer and lacking discipline, I didn't make much progress at that time. However, my determination to learn programming led me to discover a mobile application called Termux. I started using it to learn and run C programs from the 100k challenge. This mobile app provided me with a platform to practice coding even without a computer. As time went on, I faced a roadblock in my progress with the 100k challenge. The challenge began focusing on teaching Java and its Swing library, which required a computer. Since I didn't have one, I felt stuck and unable to proceed. Despite the setback, I refused to give up. Instead, I started experimenting on my own by writing terminal-level applications in C++. One of my proudest creations during this time was a program that printed a yearly calendar. Eventually, I gained access to my uncle's old desktop computer, which had Windows 7 installed on it, although it was slowly becoming obsolete. Despite the challenges, I persevered and eventually completed the 100k challenge. I continued my self-guided learning journey by exploring topics on the internet, often staying ahead of or in sync with the teachings in my school's computer classes. During computer lab sessions, I found joy in helping my fellow students when they encountered errors or needed explanations of concepts, especially during exam preparation. This experience made me feel like an expert in computers and programming to my peers. However, it is important to note that while I excelled in programming, my academic grades did not reflect my expertise.
